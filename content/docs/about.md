## Why this exists

Every documentation team has style rules. They live in a style guide PDF that nobody opens, in a senior writer's head, or in PR comments that say "this doesn't sound right" without explaining why.

I've seen this pattern at every company I've worked at — from Amazon to Microsoft to startups. New writers don't know the rules. Reviewers catch the same issues in every review cycle. Quality depends on who's available to review, not on the standards themselves.

Generic AI writing tools don't help. They optimize for engagement, suggest vague improvements, and have no concept of your product terminology. They'll happily replace your carefully chosen technical terms with "simpler" alternatives that change the meaning.

TechWrit AI exists because style guides shouldn't just be documents — they should be enforceable systems.

## The idea

At Cigna, I built a GenAI writing toolkit that reduced feature-writing cycle time by 75%. The approach was straightforward: encode writing standards into structured AI prompts so the output follows your rules from the first draft instead of the third revision.

TechWrit AI is that idea, productized. It codifies your style rules, terminology standards, and product glossary into configuration that the AI enforces automatically — across 14 purpose-built modes for every documentation task.

## Who built it

I'm Patricia McPhee, a technical writer with 30 years in tech. I've documented APIs, SDKs, and developer platforms at Microsoft, Amazon, Facebook/Oculus, GE Healthcare, LivePerson, Beyond Identity, and Expedia Group. I specialize in developer documentation, docs-as-code workflows, and information architecture.

I built TechWrit AI because I wanted the tool I wished I'd had at every job: one that knows my style guide, understands my product terminology, and gives me measurable quality improvements instead of subjective opinions.

The 25 default style rules in TechWrit AI aren't theoretical. They're the rules I've enforced (and seen violated) across a decade of documentation reviews. Every mode, every prompt, every default is built on what actually matters in technical writing — accuracy first, write for the reader's task.
